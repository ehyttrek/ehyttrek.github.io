Flow

I am a block element of the type section. As a block element I will always claim 100% of the horizontal space available to me. In vertical direction I will become just as big as the content I carry requires, as long as no one tries to manipulate my height using CSS. This is called flow. Sometimes it is even called "natural" flow.

Overflow

I am also a section. I behave the same as my sister above, only that someone has given me the value 20vh (that is 20 percewnt of the viewport height) for my height property. You can see where I end (I am green, by the way, just like my sisters). But still my content goes further than my vertical size. This is called overflow.

Overflow Hidden

I am, as well, a section. In fact we all are the same in this aspect here on the page. We just have different flow or overflow behaviour respectively. Overflow: hidden in my case. My height is set to 20vh just like my sisters' above and below. You probably won't even see this line anymore in most cases.

Overflow Scroll

Well, same for me. I am a section with text inside (the text itself is in a paragraph of course, but that doesn't matter here now). My height also 20vh, therefore I have the overeflow issue as well, but in my case the user can scroll to see the entire text. That would be 'overflow: scroll', yes, you are right!
